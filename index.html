About Digitize

Digitize is a hackathon for high school students in the Bay Area. It will take place overnight from September 22-23. It will be held at [insert venue] from 11am to 12pm on the next day.

FAQs

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a 24 hour period of time for students to develop unique computer science projects from scratch. Groups of around four students work together to bring their ideas to life with the help of professional mentors.

Do I need a Team or an idea?

No, you will have the chance to meet new people who you can choose to work with during the hackathon. Additionally, you can brainstorm ideas for projects once you arrive, and find a team.

Why come to Digitize?

Digitize is a hackathon geared towards curious high school students who are interested in entering tech fields, but have never been to a hackathon or have little exposure to computer science. However, even if you have experience, you are still welcome! It is a great way to learn about programming and interact with professionals in the field.

How can I help?

We are always looking for speakers, mentors, workshop leaders, and sponsors. If you would like to help us out please go to check out the “Help us section” at the bottom of the page.

Can I leave early?

Yes, but you will need to inform us a week before by emailing us at general@digitizehackathon.tech. In this email, you should state the name of the parent/guardian who is picking you up and at what time you will be leaving. Additionally you will need to check out with a supervisor at the hackathon

What should I bring?

Laptop, Snacks(there will be snacks provided too), Water, Sleeping bag, pillow, blanket, Extra clothes, Your imagination!
